Technological tools

Youtube

Article 1

Concept 1: YouTube is a video-sharing platform that allows users to upload, share, and view videos.It was created
in 2005 and has since become one of the largest and most popular websites on the internet, with billions
of hours of video content being uploaded and watched every day.

Article 2

Concept 2: YouTube features a wide variety of content, from music videos and movie trailers to educational videos
and DIY tutorials. Users can create their own channels and upload videos, and viewers can subscribe to
channels to receive updates when new videos are posted.
In addition to user-generated content, YouTube also features content from established media outlets,
such as news organizations and entertainment companies. The platform is free to use, but users can pay
for premium features such as ad-free viewing and access to exclusive content.

Twitter

Article 1

Concept 1: Twitter is a social media platform where users can post short messages, known as "tweets", of up to 280
characters in length. It was launched in 2006 and has since become one of the most popular social media
sites in the world, with millions of active users.
On Twitter, users can follow other users to see their tweets, and can also "like" and "retweet" posts
that they find interesting or relevant. Twitter is often used as a platform for sharing news, opinions,
and ideas, and has been used to organize political campaigns, promote businesses, and share personal
updates.

Article 2

Concept 2: Twitter also allows users to communicate with each other directly through its direct messaging feature,
and has features such as hashtags and trending topics that allow users to track and join conversations
on specific topics.
In addition to individual users, many organizations, public figures, and celebrities use Twitter to
share news and information with their followers.

Facebook

Article 1

Concept 1: Facebook is a social networking platform that allows users to connect with friends and family, share
photos and videos, and engage with content from other users and pages. It was launched in 2004 and has
since grown to become one of the most widely-used social media sites in the world, with billions of
active users.
On Facebook, users create a personal profile wheArticle 1 can share information about themselves,
including their name, location, work and education history, interests, and relationship status. Users
can also create and join groups based on shared interests or affiliations, and can follow pages
dedicated to specific topics, businesses, or organizations.

Article 2

Concept 2: Facebook features a newsfeed where users can see updates from their friends and pages they follow, as
well as advertisements and sponsored content. Users can like, comment, and share posts, and can also
communicate with friends through Facebook Messenger, a separate messaging app.
In addition to personal use, Facebook is also used by businesses to promote their products and services,
and by organizations to communicate with their members and supporters.
